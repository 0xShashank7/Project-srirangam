Srirangam

Ranganathaswamy

Srirangam is famous for its Sri Ranganathaswamy Temple, a major pilgrimage destination for Hindus and the largest temple complex in India.
It is also considered the first, foremost and the most important of the 108 main Vishnu temples (Divyadesams).

Rajagopuram

The Sriranganathaswamy Temple is enormous in size and composed of 7 concentric walled sections and 21 towers gopuram.
The southern gopuram of the temple, called the Rajagopuram, is 239.5 feet tall and, as of 2016, is the tallest in Asia.
The construction of the Rajagopuram began during the reign of Achyuta Deva Raya of the Vijayanagara empire.

Ramanujacharya

Ramanuja (11th century), one of the most celebrated theologians of Hinduism, made his monastic home by the temple at Srirangam
Here he wrote his famous commentaries on the Brahma Sutra, which expressed a qualified non-dualism of the Vedanta, his Vishishtadvaita
Ramanuja's body is said to come out of the Earth after he was buried and was preserved at this temple.

Trichy

Srirangam resides in a city called tiruchirappalli
It is a river island bounded by the Kaveri River on one side and its distributary Kollidam on the other side
Trichy is also famous for its temple called as Ucchipuliyar temple (Rockfort). It is constructed on an 83 metres (272 ft) high rock.
